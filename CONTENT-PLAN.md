The Hero — Anatomy & Purpose
A hero section is the first 5 seconds. The visitor decides stay or leave here. Every element has one job.

The segments, in order:
1. Eyebrow
A tiny label above the headline. Purpose: orient the visitor instantly — who is this for, what category is this. It answers "am I in the right place?" before they read anything else. Usually 5-10 words, often all-caps, subdued style so it doesn't compete with the headline.

2. Headline
The single most important piece of text on the page. Purpose: make a claim so compelling they have to read the next line. It should not explain — it should arrest. One idea, stated as boldly as possible. If someone reads only this, they should walk away with the core promise.

3. Sub-headline
Purpose: earn trust by grounding the headline. The headline made a bold claim — the sub says "here's how / here's what that means concretely." This is where you can introduce the product, the mechanism, the 3 steps. It's allowed to be 2-3 sentences.

4. CTA (call to action)
Purpose: capture intent from anyone the headline converted. Not everyone who reads will act — but for those who do, this is the exit ramp. Primary CTA = the main ask. Secondary CTA = lower commitment option for the hesitant.
